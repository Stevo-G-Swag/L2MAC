Chapter 6: Sauces and Accompaniments

After mastering the art of pasta making, the next step in your culinary journey through Italy is to learn about the wide array of sauces that can transform a simple pasta dish into a masterpiece of flavors. This chapter delves into the heart of Italian cuisine by exploring the variety of sauces that complement Italian pasta, from the simplest tomato sauces to the more complex and rich ragùs. Each recipe is designed to be easy to follow, ensuring that you can recreate these traditional flavors in your own kitchen.

**Simple Tomato Sauce**

- **Ingredients**:
  - 2 tablespoons olive oil
  - 1 small onion, finely chopped
  - 2 garlic cloves, minced
  - 1 can (28 ounces) of whole peeled tomatoes
  - Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  - Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

- **Instructions**:
  1. Heat the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until soft and translucent.
  2. Crush the tomatoes and add them to the saucepan, along with their juices. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Simmer the sauce for about 20-30 minutes, or until it thickens slightly. Use a wooden spoon to break down the tomatoes as they cook.
  4. Serve over your favorite pasta, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

**Basil Pesto**

- **Ingredients**:
  - 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  - 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  - 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  - 1/3 cup pine nuts
  - 2 garlic cloves
  - Salt and freshly ground black pepper

- **Instructions**:
  1. Combine the basil, Parmesan, olive oil, pine nuts, and garlic in a food processor. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Blend until smooth.
  3. Serve with pasta, ideally with shapes that can hold the sauce well, such as fusilli or trofie.

**Classic Ragù**

- **Ingredients**:
  - 2 tablespoons olive oil
  - 1 onion, finely chopped
  - 1 carrot, finely chopped
  - 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  - 1 pound ground beef
  - 1/2 pound ground pork
  - 1 cup red wine
  - 1 can (28 ounces) of whole peeled tomatoes
  - Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  - 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped

- **Instructions**:
  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrot, and celery, and sauté until they are soft.
  2. Increase the heat to high and add the ground beef and pork. Cook until browned.
  3. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer until reduced by half.
  4. Add the tomatoes and their juices, breaking them up with a spoon. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Simmer the sauce over low heat for 1-2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  6. Stir in the parsley before serving. This sauce pairs well with wider pasta like tagliatelle or pappardelle.
